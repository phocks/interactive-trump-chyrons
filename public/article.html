Look who's still talking about Trump

Interactive Digital Storytelling team

Posted November 08, 2017 16:31:10

Donald Trump is an expert at media manipulation, and a look at US news channels one year after his election shows he's still attracting an extraordinary amount of attention.

One way of checking out what the news channels are talking about is to analyse their chyrons: the text that takes up the bottom of the screen during programs.

full trump chart minus legend

This chart tracks mentions of Trump's name in news program chyrons over the last few months. On X number of days, he is talked about more than 50 per cent of the time on two of the networks. That's more Trump than all of the other news put together.

Of three major US channels, which one regularly peaks at or near 50pc Trump-talk, and which mentions Trump the least?

Surprisingly, it's left-leaning MSNBC that is most obsessed with the president, and right-leaning Fox News that mentions him the least.

reveal legend

Let's look at some of the events that shaped peaks and troughs in mentions.

On some days, a tsunami of Trump-related stories pushes his chunk of airtime above 50pc on two of the networks.

MSNBC picks up stories from the New York Times and CNN on September 18, when the network spends 60pc of its programs talking about the president.

The BBC chooses to cover more non-Trump stories than the US channels, but even the Brits find Trump taking over 20 per cent of their time on September 19 - the day of his 'Rocket Man' speech to the UN.

Many other major stories are unfolding: California bushfires, Puerto Rico's hurricane devastation and Harvey Weinstein sexual harassment allegations.
